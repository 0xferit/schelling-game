Meet Thomas
Schelling

In 1960, economist Thomas Schelling published The Strategy of Conflict — a landmark study in how people coordinate without communicating. He found that in the absence of explicit agreement, people naturally gravitate toward answers that feel salient or prominent. He called these focal points.

This game is a live experiment of that theory.

Incentive Layer / 05 From focal-point logic to live incentive design Why Ethereum can use it

Why the incentive
holds

The Schelling Game turns focal-point theory into a playable incentive structure. The match works because incentives, timing, and settlement all push players toward the same obvious answer instead of toward manipulation.

These are the same conditions that later make Schelling-style coordination usable on chain. Without them, the mechanic stays a toy instead of becoming infrastructure.

Ethereum
Context

In 2014, Vitalik Buterin proposed SchellingCoin: an early design for using Schelling-point incentives on a blockchain. Reward the report that matches the majority, and honest reporting becomes the best strategy available.

That logic now sits underneath oracle designs, curation markets, and governance systems across Ethereum whenever decentralized truth is needed without a trusted authority.

Applications

Once truthful convergence becomes the rational move, the same mechanic can settle prices, outcomes, disputes, and governance choices without a trusted operator sitting above the system.
